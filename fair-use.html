1. Introduction

This Fair Use Policy (“Policy”) applies to Backyard Bandwidth’s VPN, VPS, S3-compatible storage, colocation, and any other related services (collectively, the “Service”). By using the Service, you agree to comply with this Policy and our Terms.

Translation: we built this so normal users don’t get wrecked by one person running a 24/7 firehose.

2. VPN Fair Use

Backyard Bandwidth offers VPN service without arbitrary daily caps or “speed tiers” for typical usage. That said, VPN usage must remain reasonable. If your sustained usage materially degrades network performance, harms other users, triggers upstream abuse workflows, or creates undue infrastructure load, we may take action.

Privacy statement (read this carefully):

We do not log, track, or inspect user activity inside your VPN tunnel (no browsing history, no payload inspection). See our Logging Policy and Privacy Policy.

What we can see: infrastructure-level signals required to operate the service (e.g., aggregate traffic volume, connection health, abuse complaints, and inbound attacks targeting our systems). We use those signals to keep the lights on—not to snoop.

Not allowed on VPN:
  • Operating open relays / public proxies for third parties.
  • Running large-scale automated abuse (spam, credential stuffing, scanning that generates complaints, etc.).
  • Anything that causes repeated upstream provider abuse escalations. (We don’t enjoy those emails.)

4. VPS Privacy & Data Retention

We do not monitor or inspect activity within your VPS. You are responsible for what you run. When a VPS is terminated, data is deleted. We do not keep “archives” of terminated instances.

Legal compliance

You must comply with all applicable United States laws and any applicable laws in your jurisdiction. Illegal activity may result in termination without notice.

Important nuance (because people get confused):

We don’t “watch your screen.” But if your instance is generating abuse at the network edge (e.g., scanning floods, repeated authentication attacks against external services, spam bursts), upstream providers may complain. If that happens repeatedly, we will act.

5. S3-Compatible Storage Fair Use

We offer S3-compatible storage with free ingress and free egress under fair use terms. The goal is to be useful, not to subsidize someone’s CDN business.

  • Users may egress (download) data without charge up to the amount of data they store.
  • If a user egresses more data than they store, additional fees or restrictions may apply.
  • We may rate-limit or suspend access if usage patterns materially impact stability or trigger upstream issues.
Example:

If you store 5 TB and egress 5 TB, cool. If you store 50 GB and try to egress 30 TB, you’re not “saving money” — you’re getting rate-limited.

6. Bruteforce Attempts & Security Violations

We enforce a strict policy against bruteforce attacks targeting any part of our infrastructure, including: login portals, APIs, VPN gateways, and storage systems.

Definition

A bruteforce attempt is an automated or repeated attempt to gain unauthorized access by systematically guessing usernames, passwords, SSH keys, or API tokens.

  • Rapid or scripted login attempts across multiple accounts or services
  • Credential stuffing / password spraying
  • Scanning specifically to identify authentication surfaces for attack
Privacy clarification

We do not inspect activity inside your VPN tunnel or inside your VPS. Enforcement here is based on visible inbound behavior against our systems, plus abuse reputation signals. See our Logging Policy.

If you bruteforce our stuff:
  • Your account may be terminated immediately without notice.
  • No refunds for services terminated due to this violation.
  • We may report malicious attempts to upstream providers or appropriate authorities.

If it’s a genuine misconfiguration (broken cron, bad script) and there’s no malicious intent and no impact, we may warn you first. We are not obligated to.

7. Termination & Enforcement

If you violate this Policy, we may (in our sole discretion) restrict, rate-limit, suspend, or terminate your access without prior notice. We may also require plan changes or impose technical mitigations to protect the network.

We typically try to be reasonable.

Accidental or short-term spikes generally aren’t a problem. Repeated or sustained abuse is. If network integrity is at risk, we will prioritize stability over your feelings.

8. No Refunds for Abuse

Services terminated due to a violation of this Policy are not eligible for refunds. That includes partial months, prepayments, and “but I didn’t mean it” situations.

12. Network Bandwidth Fair Use

All services that use our network infrastructure—including VPN, VPS, S3 storage, or any other networked service—are subject to fair use limits on sustained bandwidth consumption.

Normal is fine:

Large updates, backups, short spikes, bursty transfers—fine. Long-term sustained high throughput that degrades the network—not fine.

VPS bandwidth limits (per instance)
  • Tiny (1 GB RAM / 1 CPU / 20 GB disk): up to 10 GB/day egress and 200 GB/month total traffic
  • Small (4 GB RAM / 4 CPU / 100 GB disk): 75 GB/day egress, 500 GB/week total
  • Medium (16 GB RAM / 8 CPU / 200 GB disk): 150 GB/day egress, 1.2 TB/week total
  • Large (32 GB RAM / 16 CPU / 400 GB disk): 300 GB/day egress, 2.5 TB/week total
  • Huge (64 GB RAM / 32 CPU / 1 TB disk): 600 GB/day egress, 5 TB/week total
  • Massive (128 GB RAM / 40 CPU / 2 TB disk): 1 TB/day egress, 10 TB/week total

Note: The Tiny plan is intentionally constrained to prevent accidental overuse on the lowest tier. If you need sustained high throughput, ask for a custom plan.

Enforcement approach:

Accidental or short-term overages typically won’t trigger enforcement. Repeated or continuous misuse may. We may warn you, rate-limit you, require an upgrade, or terminate service if network integrity is impacted.
