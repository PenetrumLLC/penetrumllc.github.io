Warrant Canary Policy

Last updated: July 14th, 2025

Backyard Bandwidth maintains a regularly updated warrant canary to signal that we have not received any secret legal demands, such as FISA court orders, National Security Letters (NSLs), or gag orders. Should this canary be altered, omitted, or stop updating, it should be interpreted as a silent indication that such a demand may have been received.

1. Update Schedule

The warrant canary is updated automatically every 8 hours. If it is not updated for more than 24 hours, it should be considered invalid.

2. Canary Location

The canary JSON can be found at: https://backyardbandwidth.com/data/canary.json

3. Canary Contents

The JSON file includes:

  • Human-readable timestamp
  • Epoch timestamp
  • Signed canary message
  • PGP key ID, UID, and full fingerprint

4. Public Key

Our public key can be downloaded here:

https://backyardbandwidth.com/data/pubkey.asc

Fingerprint:

41EB 31FB 76DC 55B9 E702 6BFD FAC5 F685 248A C91C

5. Verification Instructions

To verify the canary:

# 1. Import our public key
curl https://backyardbandwidth.com/data/pubkey.asc | gpg --import

# 2. Download canary JSON and detached signature
curl -O https://backyardbandwidth.com/data/canary.json
curl -O https://backyardbandwidth.com/data/canary.sig

# 3. Verify the signature
gpg --verify canary.sig canary.json

6. What It Means If the Canary Fails

If the canary is not updated on schedule, or the signature fails verification, you should assume Backyard Bandwidth may have received a classified legal order and is legally prohibited from disclosing it.

7. Disclaimer

Warrant canaries are not guaranteed legal protection under U.S. law. This mechanism is provided in good faith as a transparency tool and may not be upheld by courts in all jurisdictions.
