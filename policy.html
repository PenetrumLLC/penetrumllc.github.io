VPS Privacy Policy

Backyard Bandwidth does not log, track, or monitor user activity on our VPS services. Users are not subject to tracking under our Privacy Policy. Once a VPS has been terminated, all data associated with the VPS is permanently and forensically deleted.

While using our VPS service, we do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties, except as required by law or necessary to provide our services.

To ensure compliance with our Fair Use Policy, we collect and retain basic network usage statistics, including total ingress and egress volumes per VPS instance. These usage graphs are used solely to monitor bandwidth consumption and detect policy violations. They do not include logs of user activity or content, and are not shared externally. Once a VPS is deleted or removed, the associated usage graph is also permanently deleted from our systems.

VPN Privacy Policy

Backyard Bandwidth does not log, track, or store any data related to VPN usage. Specifically, we do not:

  • Log users' traffic or the content of any communications.
  • Log which websites you visit.
  • Store your original or assigned IP address.
  • Log session durations or timestamps.
  • Track or collect location-based data.

Additionally, all VPN data is never stored or retained in any form. All VPN traffic is encrypted, ensuring your online activity remains private and secure.

Some IP addresses assigned to our VPN services may be leased from third-party providers, and Backyard Bandwidth may not have direct ownership of these addresses. However, we do not log any data on these providers' IPs and use a modified version of our VPN server to enforce our strict no-logging policy across all assigned IP addresses.
